2. Eligibility

  • The lead applicant must be a U.S.-based individual, team, organization, or community group, with a clearly identified lead applicant who is 21 years of age or older at the time of submission.
  • The proposed project must serve adults 21 and older within a defined U.S. community. Projects primarily serving individuals under 21 are not eligible.
  • The project must be in early-stage, pilot, or scaling phase. Fully self-sustaining or mature programs are not eligible.
  • The project must not involve lobbying, partisan political activity, electioneering, or individual cash assistance.
  • The project must not involve restricted categories including tobacco, nicotine, cannabis, alcohol, gambling, firearms, or similar regulated industries.

4. Judging

Submissions are independently judged by a panel of nationally acclaimed leaders convened for the Program. Each submission is scored on a 0–10 scale across five weighted dimensions totaling 100%: Community Impact (25%), Feasibility (20%), Innovation (20%), Sustainability (20%), and Founder/Team (15%). The selection process includes Eligibility Review, Scored Review, Finalist Selection, and Verification. Decisions of the judging panel are final and not subject to appeal.

5. Awards

  • Five winning teams will each receive a grant of $50,000 USD, totaling $250,000 across the Program.
  • Grants are unrestricted, but recipients are expected to deliver against the project plan submitted in their application and to participate in light reporting and storytelling moments tied to the Program through December 2027.
  • Funds may not be used for lobbying, political contributions, restricted categories listed in Section 2, or activities outside the approved project scope.
  • Recipients are responsible for all applicable taxes. The Program funder (PMI U.S. or its designated grantmaking entity) will issue an IRS Form 1099 where required.
