Ideas are the origin. Action is the engine. Communities are the proof.

America's first 250 years were powered by bold, disruptive ideas. What comes next is being built now by the people moving America forward.

PMI U.S. is invested in America — and in the people and projects shaping what's next. In 2026, to honor America's 250th anniversary and spirit of innovation, PMI U.S. is funding the Community Futures Challenge, a national innovation platform designed in partnership with Arizona State University, to uncover America's boldest ideas — and help turn them into reality, anywhere in the country.

Questions, answered.

Who is eligible to apply?

The nationwide search is open to any U.S.-based innovator, community leader, entrepreneur, or changemaker — aged 21 and up — tackling one of America's toughest problems. Applicants may be individuals, teams, or organizations. The project must serve adults 21 and older within a defined U.S. community. Early-stage, pilot, and scaling projects qualify; fully self-sustaining initiatives do not.

What kinds of projects are you looking for?

You name it. Workforce development. Economic mobility. Public infrastructure. Civic technology. Entrepreneurship. Anything that materially strengthens a U.S. community. We favor the specific, the replicable, and the measurable over the broadly aspirational.

When do submissions open and close?

Submissions open June 2026 and close November 2026. Finalists are notified in January 2027. The five winners are announced and recognized at a dedicated event in Phoenix, AZ in February 2027.

How are proposals judged?

An independent panel of nationally acclaimed judges scores entries across five dimensions, each on a 0–10 scale weighted by importance: community impact (25%), feasibility (20%), innovation (20%), sustainability (20%), and founder/team (15%) — 100% in total. The full panel is published on this site under Judges.

Are there restrictions on how the $50,000 is used?

The grant is unrestricted, but recipients are expected to deliver against the project plan submitted in their application and to participate in light reporting and storytelling moments tied to the program.
